Masuria Lake District

Giżycko

Giżycko is sailing capital of Poland. It is situated between Lake Kisajno and Lake Niegocin in the region of Masuria. Giżycko is a popular summer tourist destination due to its location within the Masurian Lake District and possesses numerous historical monuments, including a 14th-century Teutonic castle.

Mikołajki

Mikołajki is a resort town in Mrągowo County in the Warmian-Masurian Voivodeship in north-eastern Poland. The town is located near the Śniardwy, the largest lake of both the Masurian Lake District and Poland. It is located in the center of the ethnocultural region of Masuria.

Wolf's Lair

The Wolf's Lair was the seat of Adolf Hitler. The quarter was built so that he could command the troops conquering the USSR. On July 20, 1944 Claus von Stauffenberg and Werner von Haeften made an unsuccessful attempt on Hitler's life here

Guzianka

The Guzianka Lock is located between Lake Bełdany and lake Little buttons. It was put into operation in 1900, that is over a hundred years ago, and it works for a day. The Guzianka Lock is a very infrared node on the route of the Great Masurian Lakes. The water stand of the activity of the city of Ruciane-Nida on the Nidzkie Lake with the result of a navigable route. During the season, several hundred yachts, motorboats, kayaks and other floating objects are crossed here every day. They also use the white fleet.

Mrągowo

Mrągowo is a city famous for its amphitheater located on Lake Czos. The Mazurska Noc Kabaretowa takes place here every year. There is also the Museum of Military Equipment in Mrągowo. Some vehicles were used in the 9th Communication Specialist Training Center in Mrągowo.

Węgorzewo

In Węgorzewo, there is a Teutonic Castle from 1398, rebuilt in the first half of the 18th century in the Baroque style. In the mid-19th century, the castle was converted into a court and a prison. After the destruction during World War II, it was rebuilt and intended for cultural purposes.

Śniardwy Lake

At 113.8 square kilometres, Śniardwy is the largest lake in Poland. It was also the largest lake in Prussia, when Warmia-Masuria was under German rule. It is 22.1 kilometres long and 13.4 kilometres wide. The maximum depth is 23 metres. There are eight islands on the Śniardwy lake.

Swing Bridge in Giżycko

A bridge weighing more than 100 tons by means of a gear user can be force rotated by a single operator. This is where the sailing season kicks off and there are parades with decorated boats every year.
